Optimizely (formerly Episerver) sits at the top of the enterprise CMS market. It is a full Digital Experience Platform — combining content management, A/B testing, personalisation, commerce, and analytics under one roof. Gartner puts it in the Leaders quadrant. It is used by large enterprises with large budgets.

If that is what your organisation needs, this comparison will likely confirm Optimizely is on the right shortlist. If you are a team that needs excellent headless content management with editorial workflow and compliance controls — but not an enterprise DXP contract — then the gap in value becomes interesting.

## Pricing

Optimizely does not publish pricing. Enterprise DXP contracts typically run between €50,000 and €200,000+ per year, and that excludes implementation costs — which, with the partner ecosystem required, routinely add six figures more.

Papevi's Scale plan at €199/month (€1,990/year) covers multi-site, full workflow, audit log, and GDPR controls. The Enterprise plan adds custom limits, dedicated support, NIS-2 controls, and SLA on a custom contract — but the baseline capability is available to any team without a sales process.

**Bottom line:** If you need A/B testing, personalisation, and an enterprise DXP, Optimizely's price point is justified by its breadth. If you need a structured headless CMS with real editorial workflow and compliance, you are paying for features you will never use.

## Editorial workflow

This is where Optimizely genuinely earns its position. Approval routing, content scheduling, version comparison, multi-site publishing, and sophisticated role management are all well-implemented and have been refined over many years.

Papevi covers the same core workflow surface: draft → review → approved → published, configurable approval routing, inline comment threads, scheduled publish and unpublish, side-by-side revision comparison, and one-click restore. The difference is availability: these features are on Papevi's Scale plan at €199/month.

**Bottom line:** The workflow feature sets are comparable for most teams. Papevi will not give you Optimizely's personalisation or experiment management, but for content workflow alone, the gap is small.

## Developer experience

Optimizely offers both REST and GraphQL APIs, and has a mature SDK ecosystem. It integrates with the broader Optimizely platform — experimentation, data platform, commerce — which matters if you are buying the full suite.

Papevi offers a clean JSON:API-based REST API. The API is straightforward to consume from any stack and is fully documented. For teams that do not need the Optimizely platform integrations, the API experience is comparable.

**Bottom line:** If your team is already in the Optimizely ecosystem, the platform integrations are valuable. If you are evaluating on API quality alone, Papevi competes well.

## Compliance and security

Both platforms offer GDPR controls, role-based access, and audit trails. Optimizely has EU hosting options. Papevi stores all data in the EU by default, with GDPR media controls and DPA available on request from the Scale plan.

NIS-2 compliance controls are available on Papevi Enterprise.

**Bottom line:** Neither platform has an edge here for regulated use cases. The difference is that Papevi includes these controls at a transparent price point without requiring an enterprise negotiation.

## Implementation

Getting Optimizely live typically requires a certified partner or agency. Projects routinely take several months and substantial professional services investment. Larger organisations often find this justified — the implementation partner also builds the frontend, integrations, and governance processes.

Papevi is self-service. You can have a site, a content model, and your first published entry within hours. The API documentation covers every endpoint, and no certification is required to get started.

**Bottom line:** If your organisation needs guidance, implementation, and ongoing platform management from a partner, Optimizely has a large ecosystem for that. If your team can operate independently, Papevi's self-service model saves months and hundreds of thousands in implementation costs.

## When to choose Papevi

- You need headless CMS with editorial workflow and compliance, not a full DXP
- Budget is a real constraint and you cannot justify a six-figure platform contract
- Your team wants to be operational in days, not months
- You do not need A/B testing, personalisation, or commerce built into the CMS layer

## When Optimizely may be the right choice

- Your organisation is already invested in the Optimizely ecosystem (experimentation, CDP, commerce)
- Personalisation and A/B testing are core to your content strategy
- You need a certified partner to lead implementation and ongoing management
- You are a large enterprise for whom the contract cost is proportionate to the business value
